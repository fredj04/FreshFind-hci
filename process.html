Six Evidence-Based Findings (Phase 2)

F1
Stale data causes wasted tripsUsers arrive at pantries listed as open only to find them closed. This is the #1 reason people abandon food-finding apps.Source: Reddit/r/foodbank · App reviews (Plentiful)
F2
Route-based discovery, not just proximityRoutine persistence means people shop along commutes, not near home. "Nearest resource" filters are insufficient.Source: NotebookLM Synthesis (Routine Persistence section)
F3
The Food MirageNearby stores exist but prices are unaffordable. Dollar stores and fast food saturate neighborhoods. SNAP/EBT tags are essential from v1.Source: NotebookLM Synthesis · Reddit/r/povertyfinance
F4
Transit deserts overlap food desertsMap distance is meaningless without transit data. Carrying capacity (bags, children) adds additional constraints.Source: Feeding America · NotebookLM Synthesis
F5
Stigma suppresses use of visible resourcesUsers described relief when no ID or account was required. Shame affects whether people seek assistance at all.Source: Reddit/r/SNAP · Needfinding Guide Category 4
F6
Community trust beats formal listingsCOVID-era mutual aid proved communities share accurate food info when given easy tools. Frictionless anonymous updates are the core trust mechanism.Source: Feeding America 2023 · Reddit community threads

AI Agent vs. NotebookLM Comparison

The AI Needfinding Agent generated hypotheses that were then checked against real sources in NotebookLM. Three of four agent hypotheses were contradicted or only partially supported.

AI Agent HypothesisNotebookLM FindingResultDesign Implication
Recipes and dietary filters are the primary needUsers need real-time status and SNAP tags far more than recipesContradictedDrop recipes from v1; prioritize SNAP filters
Real-time accuracy is "nice to have"Stale data is the #1 reason for app abandonment (F1)ContradictedLive timestamps are a core feature, not optional
Show nearest resource as defaultRoutine persistence: proximity alone is insufficientPartially supportedAdd route-based "Stop on My Way" alongside proximity
Identity-based reviews build trustAccount creation drives abandonment; anonymity builds trustContradictedAll contributions anonymous — no login ever

Three Design Tasks

Task 1

Check If a Pantry Is Open

User: Joe, South Bronx, heading home from work.
Addresses F1 (stale data), F4 (transit distance), F6 (community trust).

Task 2

Switch App Language

User: Marie, first-time Spanish-speaking user.
Addresses language access barrier, F3 affordability-first UX.

Task 3

Submit a Status Update

User: Steven, leaving a pantry that ran out of produce.
Addresses F1 (accuracy), F5 (anonymity), F6 (community as trust).
