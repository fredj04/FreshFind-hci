Project Overview

FreshFind is a mobile application designed to give residents of food deserts real-time access to food resources — including food pantries, soup kitchens, mobile produce vans, and street vendors. The app is built around community-powered, anonymous status updates, multilingual support, and low-bandwidth optimization for the populations who need it most.

Three Primary Tasks

Task 1 · Real-Time Accuracy

Check If a Pantry Is Open

Live color-coded map with community-verified timestamps — no more wasted trips on outdated information.

Task 2 · Equitable Access

Switch App Language

First-launch language picker in 7 languages. The entire app — listings, filters, community notes — renders in the selected language.

Task 3 · Community Contribution

Submit a Status Update

One-tap anonymous status update. No account, no login. Under 10 seconds. Updates are live immediately for everyone nearby.

Design Process Phases

Phase 1

Problem Definition

Identified food deserts as the core problem: 19M Americans, including 1.3M NYC residents, lack reliable access to fresh food. Existing tools (USDA Atlas, Google Maps) are outdated or incomplete.

Phase 2

Design Research

6 evidence-based findings from Reddit, Feeding America, app reviews, and NotebookLM synthesis. AI Agent vs. NotebookLM comparison revealed 3 contradicted hypotheses. Three tasks defined.

Phase 3

High-Fidelity Prototyping

12 screens across 3 task flows. Heuristic evaluation by peer team (FoodPath) found 10 issues. All severity 2+ issues resolved in final prototype.

Key Principles

Non-Negotiable Design Values

Real-time accuracy · Zero login required · Anonymous contributions · Multilingual from first launch · Low-bandwidth optimization · Dignity-preserving language
